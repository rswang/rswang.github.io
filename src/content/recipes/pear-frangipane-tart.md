---
title: Pear Frangipane Tart
image: /images/recipes/pear-frangipane-tart.jpg
date: 2026-06-27
blurb: Almond cream + pears in a buttery crust
story:
  - "My mom's favorite dessert from our local French bakery. When it shut down, she asked me to learn how to make it, and it's become a staple ever since."
  - "It comes together from three simple parts: a press-in French tart shell, an almond frangipane, and a can of pear halves fanned on top. Don't fuss over the pears — even a rough fan looks lovely once it bakes up golden."
ingredients:
  - — For the tart shell —
  - 85g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  - 1 tbsp (15ml) vegetable oil
  - 3 tbsp (45ml) water
  - 1 tbsp (15g) sugar
  - ⅛ tsp salt
  - 160g flour
  - — For the frangipane —
  - 75g blanched slivered almonds (or almond flour)
  - 1 tbsp (10g) flour
  - 90g sugar
  - 85g unsalted butter, at room temperature
  - 1 large egg
  - ½ tsp almond extract (or 2 tsp brandy), optional
  - — To assemble —
  - 1 can pear halves (about 410g), drained and patted dry
  - Powdered sugar, for dusting (optional)
steps:
  - "Make the shell: Heat oven to 210°C (410°F). In an ovenproof bowl, combine the butter, oil, water, sugar, and salt. Bake about 15 minutes, until the butter is bubbling and just starting to brown at the edges."
  - Carefully remove the bowl (it may sputter) and reduce the oven to 190°C (375°F). Tip in the flour and stir quickly until it comes together into a ball that pulls away from the sides.
  - Spread the warm dough into a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Once cool enough to handle, press it evenly across the bottom and up the sides. Prick the base all over with a fork.
  - Bake the shell about 15 minutes at 190°C, until golden brown. Let cool, then reduce the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  - "Make the frangipane: Finely grind the almonds with the flour in a food processor. Mix in the sugar, then the butter and almond extract, and blend until smooth. Mix in the egg. Cover and chill at least 3 hours."
  - "Assemble: Spread the frangipane evenly into the cooled shell. Thinly slice each pear half crosswise, gently fan the slices, and arrange them on top like the spokes of a wheel, narrow ends toward the center."
  - Bake at 180°C (350°F) for about 55 minutes, until golden and a tester inserted in the center of the filling comes out clean. Cool before serving; dust with powdered sugar if you like.
